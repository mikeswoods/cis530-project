MUCH of the tax agenda that Republicans promised in the ''Contract With America'' has been enacted into law in the last five years. But as Newt Gingrich, the contract's author, is fond of saying: There is no free lunch.
The contract's new tax breaks for middle-class parents, investors, the self-employed and others have come at a price that millions of Americans are discovering as they engage in the annual ritual of filing their income tax returns: a complexity that might make even Rube Goldberg indignant.
