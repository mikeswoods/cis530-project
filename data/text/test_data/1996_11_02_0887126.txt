In the most dramatic foray ever by a foreign company into the American communications business, British Telecommunications could announce as early as tomorrow that it has agreed to buy MCI, the nation's second-largest long distance company, in a deal worth more than $22 billion, people close to the situation said last evening.
If the acquisition is approved by the boards of both companies in meetings today, it would be the first time a foreign company has taken control of a major American telephone company. In fact, it would be the largest foreign takeover of any United States corporation, coming in a year when mergers and acquisitions are at a record-setting pace.  $(Page 37.$).
