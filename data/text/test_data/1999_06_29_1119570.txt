One of the mysteries about Martin R. Frankel, the man authorities suspect of orchestrating a spectacular insurance fraud, is how a financier who almost never left his well-guarded Greenwich, Conn., mansion befriended corporate and religious elites on two continents.
The answer appears to be that Mr. Frankel turned to Thomas J. Corbally, a business consultant whose ties to powerful people made him a valuable contributor at Kroll Associates, one of America's best-known private investigative agencies.
