IF the prospect of leasing rather than buying a new car has you cowering in confusion, you are not alone.
While car leasing has become popular, it is often impossible to determine the true costs because the contracts have never had to follow strict disclosure rules. Many consumers simply assume they are paying a relatively paltry sum -- say $199 a month over three years -- to lease a car. But what are the actual lease charges, as well as the car's basic price? And how much do consumers have to pay if they end leases early?
