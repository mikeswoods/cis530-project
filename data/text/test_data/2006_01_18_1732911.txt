Intel, the world's largest chip maker, said slower sales of desktop computers and weaker prices led it to miss its revenue target for the fourth quarter, causing the stock to decline more than 9 percent in late trading on Tuesday.
The disappointing earnings report made for a rocky start to the year, coming as Yahoo also missed targets on Tuesday, causing technology stocks to decline across the board. I.B.M.'s shares, however, managed to hold steady in after-hours trading, as that company reported solid profit growth for the quarter. [Page C6.]
