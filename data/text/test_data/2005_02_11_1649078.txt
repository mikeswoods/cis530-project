Now that President Bush has put forward a proposal to allow younger workers to divert part of their Social Security payments to investment accounts, the even bigger challenge awaiting him is to offer a plan for restoring the system to financial balance.
At the top of the agenda, officials and outside experts say, is the idea of curtailing the growth of the benefits promised to future retirees by tying them to increases in consumer prices rather than wages. Prices generally rise more slowly.
