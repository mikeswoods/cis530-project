The economy ended the first quarter on a high note, according to two Commerce Department reports released yesterday, but economists cautioned that higher energy costs and interest rates might sap some of that vitality.
Two measures of housing and manufacturing activity rose sharply in March, the Commerce Department said, indicating that the economy picked up steam. The Federal Reserve's beige book report presented a similar upbeat view but noted that higher energy prices were hurting businesses and that home sales were cooling in much of the country.
