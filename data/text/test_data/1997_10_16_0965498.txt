Shoppers increased spending for the fourth straight month in September, but much of the gain appeared to reflect higher automobile prices and masked sales declines at clothing and department stores, Government figures showed today.
Analysts said the three-tenths of 1 percent increase in September retail sales reported by the Commerce Department, combined with upward revisions for July and August, portrayed an economy that clearly was decelerating. ''As the third quarter progressed, things were slowing down,'' said L. Douglas Lee of HSBC Securities in Washington. ''And I think the fourth quarter will be a slower quarter than the third.''
