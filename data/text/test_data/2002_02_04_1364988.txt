EXCEPT for the smoke coming from the basement windows, Alan Ellman led what could be considered a normal middle-class childhood in Valley Stream, N.Y., in the 1950's and 60's.
Mr. Ellman's father, a dentist with an inventive bent, turned the basement into a skunkworks, perfecting dental equipment that used radio frequencies. This sometimes caused little fires or, more often, made the TV reception go haywire during ''Gilligan's Island.''
