AT last, we're done with the holidays, that special time of year when we blow the returns from our investments on knickknacks and gewgaws for family and friends.
For those of us who don't really enjoy shopping -- and by ''us'' I mean ''guys,'' or, more specifically, me -- a strange transformation takes place around December. We think of almost every object in terms of its potential as a gift. We find ourselves in stores, looking at things and realizing how little we know about the likes and dislikes of the people in our lives.
