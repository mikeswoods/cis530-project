No longer the heroes of the stock market boom, investment bankers have seen their reputations sullied in recent months by a series of scandals, ranging from the ethical practices of securities analysts to their involvement in the Enron fiasco. Their standing may hit a new low next week, when BNP Paribas, the giant French bank, introduces an advertising campaign that compares bankers to insects.
'''What do you want, a banker or a locust?'' is the tag line from one advertisement in the series that will begin appearing in newspapers and magazines June 11. A field of corn that has been ravaged by locusts is pictured in the background. The head of a giant grinning locust peers from a banker's suit, complete with red-and-white- striped tie.
